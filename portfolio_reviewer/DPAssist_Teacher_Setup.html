Cloud Setup Guide

DPAssist for Teachers

How to set up your own automated portfolio feedback system — no coding required.

One-time setup — about 60 minutes
What you will set up
  • 1 Google Cloud Project
  • 2 Google Sheets API
  • 3 Service Account
  • 4 Share your Google Sheet
  • 5 Gmail App Password
  • 6 Gemini API Key
  • 7 Deploy to Streamlit
  • 8 Daily use
Before you begin: You will need a Gmail account, a Google Form already collecting student portfolio submissions, and the Google Sheet linked to that form. Have these ready before starting.
1

Create a Google Cloud Project

~10 min

Google Cloud is where you give DPAssist permission to read your Google Sheet. Think of it as creating a key that only your app can use.

  1. 1
    Go to Google Cloud Console
    Open your browser and go to console.cloud.google.com. Sign in with your Google account.
  2. 2
    Create a new project
    At the top of the page, click where it says "Select a project". Then click "New Project" in the top right of the popup.
  3. 3
    Name your project
    Type DPAssist as the project name and click "Create". Wait a few seconds for it to be created.
  4. 4
    Make sure your project is selected
    At the top of the page, confirm "DPAssist" is showing as the selected project.
2

Enable the Google Sheets API

~3 min

This gives your project permission to read and write to Google Sheets.

  1. 1
    Open the API Library
    In the left menu, click "APIs & Services" then "Library".
  2. 2
    Enable Google Sheets API
    Search for "Google Sheets API", click on it, then click the blue "Enable" button.
  3. 3
    Enable Google Drive API
    Go back to the Library, search for "Google Drive API", click it, and click "Enable".
3

Create a Service Account

~10 min

A service account is like a robot assistant that has its own email address and can be given permission to access your sheet. DPAssist uses this to read your student submissions.

  1. 1
    Go to Credentials
    In the left menu, click "APIs & Services" then "Credentials".
  2. 2
    Create a Service Account
    Click "+ Create Credentials" at the top, then choose "Service Account".
  3. 3
    Name it
    Type dpassist-reader as the name. Click "Create and Continue", then skip the optional steps and click "Done".
  4. 4
    Download the credentials file
    Click on the service account you just created. Go to the "Keys" tab. Click "Add Key" → "Create New Key". Choose "JSON" and click "Create". A file will download automatically — keep this file safe, you will need it in Step 7.
Important: The downloaded JSON file contains your private credentials. Never share it with anyone or post it online. Store it somewhere safe on your computer.
Find your service account email: Open the downloaded JSON file in any text editor (TextEdit on Mac, Notepad on Windows). Look for the line that says "client_email" — copy that email address. It looks like: dpassist-reader@your-project.iam.gserviceaccount.com. You will need this in Step 4.
4

Share your Google Sheet

~2 min

Give your service account access to the Google Sheet where your form responses are collected.

  1. 1
    Open your Google Sheet
    This is the sheet connected to your Google Form — the one that collects student portfolio submissions.
  2. 2
    Click Share
    Click the "Share" button in the top right corner of the sheet.
  3. 3
    Add the service account
    Paste the service account email address from Step 3 into the "Add people" box. Set permission to "Editor". Uncheck "Notify people" and click "Share".
What this looks like

Share
Find your Sheet ID: Look at the web address of your Google Sheet. It looks like:

https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/THIS-PART-IS-YOUR-ID/edit

Copy just the long string of letters and numbers between /d/ and /edit. Save it — you will need it in Step 7.
5

Create a Gmail App Password

~5 min

DPAssist sends feedback emails to students using your Gmail. An App Password is a special one-time password that lets it do this safely — without sharing your real password.

  1. 1
    Go to your Google Account
    Go to myaccount.google.com and click "Security" in the left menu.
  2. 2
    Turn on 2-Step Verification
    Under "How you sign in to Google", click "2-Step Verification" and make sure it is turned on. App Passwords require this to be active.
  3. 3
    Find App Passwords
    Search for "App passwords" in the search bar at the top of your Google Account page. Click on it when it appears.
  4. 4
    Create the password
    In the "App name" box, type DPAssist and click "Create".
  5. 5
    Copy your password
    Google will show you a 16-character password. Copy it and save it somewhere safe — Google will never show it again. Remove any spaces before saving it.
Save it now: Google shows this password only once. Copy it immediately and save it before closing the window.
6

Get a Gemini API Key

~3 min

The Gemini API is the AI brain of DPAssist. It reads student portfolios and evaluates them against your rubric. The free tier handles most classroom sizes comfortably.

  1. 1
    Go to Google AI Studio
    Go to aistudio.google.com/app/apikey and sign in with your Google account.
  2. 2
    Create an API Key
    Click "Create API Key". Copy the key that appears — it starts with "AIza" and is about 40 characters long.
  3. 3
    Save it safely
    Save the key somewhere private — treat it like a password.
7

Deploy to Streamlit Cloud

~20 min

This is the final step — deploying your own copy of DPAssist to the cloud so any teacher at your school can use it from a browser.

  1. 1
    Fork the GitHub repository
    Go to github.com/bmorrow574/DPAssist. Click the "Fork" button in the top right. This creates your own copy of the code.
  2. 2
    Go to Streamlit Cloud
    Go to share.streamlit.io and sign in with your GitHub account.
  3. 3
    Create a new app
    Click "New app". Set these fields exactly:

    Repository: your-github-name/DPAssist
    Branch: main
    Main file path: portfolio_reviewer/teacher_ui.py
  4. 4
    Open Advanced Settings → Secrets
    Click "Advanced settings" before deploying. Click "Secrets". This is where you paste all your credentials.
  5. 5
    Paste your secrets
    Type the following exactly — replacing each value with your own. Type the apostrophes manually on your keyboard.
GOOGLE_SHEET_ID = "paste-your-sheet-id-here"
GEMINI_API_KEY = "paste-your-gemini-key-here"
TEACHER_EMAIL = "your.email@gmail.com"
GMAIL_APP_PASSWORD = "your16charpassword"
RUBRIC_STORAGE = "sheets"
CHECK_INTERVAL_SECONDS = "60"
GOOGLE_CREDENTIALS_JSON = '''
paste entire contents of your service-account-credentials.json file here
'''
The triple quotes are critical: The last line uses three apostrophe characters ''' on each side of the JSON. Type these manually on your keyboard — do not copy them from a document, as some apps replace them with curved "smart quotes" that will not work.
Pasting the JSON file: Open your service-account-credentials.json file in a text editor. Press Command+A (Mac) or Ctrl+A (Windows) to select everything, then Command+C / Ctrl+C to copy. Paste it between the triple quotes in Streamlit.
  1. 6
    Click Save, then Deploy
    Click "Save" to save your secrets. Then click "Deploy". Streamlit will build and launch your app — this takes about 2 minutes.
  2. 7
    Get a clean URL (optional)
    Once deployed, click the three-dot menu next to your app → Settings → App URL. Type a name like "dpassist-yourschool" and save. Your app will be at dpassist-yourschool.streamlit.app.
You are done with setup! Your Teacher Dashboard is now live at your Streamlit URL. Bookmark it — this is all you need to open every day.
8

Daily use

2 min/day

Once set up, DPAssist runs automatically in the cloud 24/7. Here is all you need to do each day.

Adding a rubric — do this once per assignment
  • Open your Streamlit URL in any browser
  • Click "Rubrics" in the left menu
  • Upload your rubric PDF
  • Type the unit name exactly as it appears in your Google Form
  • Set the due date and click "Parse and Add Rubric"
After the deadline — reviewing scored drafts
  • Open Gmail and go to your Drafts folder
  • You will see one draft per student submission
  • Read the AI evaluation — edit anything you want to change
  • Click Send when you are happy with it
Remember: The unit name in DPAssist must exactly match the unit name in your Google Form dropdown. Capitalization matters — "Milling About" and "milling about" are treated as different units.

Your dashboard is live

Visit it in any browser — no installation, no login required for students.
